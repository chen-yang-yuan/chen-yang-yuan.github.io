About me

I am Chenyang Yuan, a first year PhD student in Biostatistics at Emory University. I am interested in research topics related to statistical genetics and genomics. Prior to joining Emory, I received my Bachelor's degree in Statistics from the Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen (CUHK-Shenzhen).

CV

This September, I am joining the Edward S. Rogers Sr. Department of Electrical & Computer Engineering at UofT, where I am going to be co-advised by Prof. Stark Draper and Prof. Wei Yu. Meanwhile, I am affiliated with the Network Coding Lab at CUHK-Shenzhen (supervisor: Prof. Shenghao Yang) and the Shenzhen Research Institute of Big Data (supervisor: Prof. Zhi-Quan Luo) as research assistants. Prior to that, I have done an internship at the Wireless Technology Laboratory, Huawei Technologies from January 2021 to May 2021.

I have gained some teaching experience as an undergraduate student teaching fellow. The courses I have taught include Calculus I, Linear Algebra, and Complex Variables and Abstract Algebra I. My typeset notes could be found here.
